# Preface

Since the 1980s, I've been deeply involved in the development of commercial Artificial Intelligence (AI) tools and applications. My journey has led me to deliver AI systems and conduct research for for organizations like Capital One, Google, SAIC, DARPA, Olive AI, PacBell, and Babylist.

## Why is this book important to you?

Too many developers and knowledge workers have started using Large Language Models (LLMS) without really understanding how they work, what their limitations are, and how best to use them for practical applications and as personal knowledge worker assistants.

My duel goal is to intertwine a solid foundation of machine learning and LLMs with practical techniques for using AI effectively in your job.

The landscape of AI has dramatically shifted. What was once the domain of physicists and computer scientists is now accessible to everyone. The latest commercial and open-source AI tools are designed with non-technical knowledge workers in mind. This democratization means you can leverage AI to enhance your productivity, creativity, and efficiency—no programming required.

As human beings, we don't live to work; we work to live. Our ultimate goal is to spend quality time with friends and family and engage in activities that nourish our souls. This book addresses a vital question:

## How can we be more efficient in our work so we have more time for what truly matters?

There has always been important decisions in out lives concerning work-life balance. Most of us felt a pull towards maximizing our earning potential and our careers while wanting to spend quality time with one family and friends.

AI technologies hold out a promise of greatly increased productivity. As I write this in April 2025 CEOs and founders of AI companies are spreading a lot of hype about the death of career software development, customer service, and other jobs 

## What this book offers

This book is your guide to navigating the exciting world of AI tools, even if you have little prior programming experience. I do expect that you, dear reader, have some experience with the Python language but this book is intended to be self-contained: if you can edit a Python source file, install libraries, and run Python scripts then you are good to go with the material in this book. This book is structured into two main parts:

You'll learn how to:

   - Automate repetitive tasks.
   - Generate insights from data.
   - Enhance your decision-making capabilities.
   - Improve communication with natural language processing.
